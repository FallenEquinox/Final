The story of Lurelin Village

Lurelin Village was spared the destruction of the Great Calamity that plagued Hyrule 100 years ago. Engraved into Zora Stone monuments, during the age of burning fields, the waters near Lurelin Village were once plagued by a giant water octorok that terrorized the village's fishermen. The Zora Prince Sidon, younger brother to the late champion Mipha, heard of the village's plight and left Zora's Domain to rid it of the monster that terrorized it. It is said that the octorok was to be as big as the mountain and swallowed many who would be heroes. After defeating the beasts, Lurelin Village survives once again and is peaceful! Lurelin Village is a seaside town located at the Western end of Faron Highroad in East Necluda. Lurelin is a coastal fishing settlement. The village is led and made possible by Rozel, a retired fisherman and elder who shares his wisdom on areas of interest. During the upheaval, villagers were forced to scatter across Hyrule to avoid the pirates that threatened their once peaceful living. When all hope was lost, a hero swooped in and saved the village, not only giving the people hope for their futures on the coast, but also the courage to open their doors to the public to experience life by the sea! Thanks to the knight's dedication, the village overflows with a playful aura and laughter.
